PBES Generation
===============

*Wieger Wesselink, Tim Willemse*

This document describes the generation of PBESs. These algorithms are implemented in the
tools **lps2pbes** and **lpsbisim2pbes**.

Transforming an LPS and a property to a PBES
---------------------------------------------

In this section we define the algorithm :math:`\operatorname{lps2pbes}` that generates a
PBES from a modal mu-calculus property :math:`\varphi` and an LPS. Let
:math:`\langle D_p, d_0, P \rangle` be the LPS given by

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lrl}
   \mathbf{proc}~P(x{:}D_p) &=&
     \sum_{i\in I}\sum_{y_i:E_i} c_i(x,y_i) \rightarrow
     a_i(f_i(x,y_i)) @ t_i(x,y_i) \cdot P(g_i(x,y_i)) \\
   &+& \sum_{j\in J}\sum_{y_j:E_j} c_j(x,y_j) \rightarrow \delta @ t_j(x,y_j);
   \end{array}

where :math:`a_i(f_i(x,y))` is a multiset of actions. Then we define

.. math::

   \operatorname{lps2pbes}(\sigma X(x_f{:}D_f{:=}d).\,\varphi,\,
   \langle D_p, d_0, P \rangle) = \mathbf{E}(\varphi),

where the function :math:`\mathbf{E}` is inductively defined using the tables below. The
function :math:`\varphi` has to be in positive normal form, i.e. it may not contain any
:math:`\lnot` or :math:`\Rightarrow` symbols. There is also an untimed variant of the
algorithm, which can be obtained by removing all time references. A formula :math:`\varphi`
not of the form :math:`\sigma X(x_f{:}D_f{:=}d).\,\varphi` is first translated into
:math:`\nu X().\,\varphi`. We assume that :math:`T{:}\mathbb{R}` is a unique fresh time
variable that is generated by the algorithm. We denote the formula :math:`\varphi` that is
passed to the algorithm :math:`\operatorname{lps2pbes}` as :math:`\varphi_0`. It is used
as a global variable in the translation.

Let :math:`a = \{a_1,\ldots,a_n\}` and :math:`b = \{b_1,\ldots,b_n\}` be two multiactions.
Let :math:`A` be the set of all permutations :math:`[i_1,\ldots,i_n]` of
:math:`[1,\ldots,n]` such that :math:`\mathit{name}(a_k) = \mathit{name}(b_{i_k})` for
:math:`k = 1\ldots n`. Then we define the function :math:`\mathbf{Sat}` as follows:

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, b) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \left\{\begin{array}{ll}
       \bigvee_{[i_1,\ldots,i_n]\in A}\bigwedge_{k=1\ldots n}(a_k = b_{i_k})
         & \text{if } A \neq \emptyset \\
       \mathit{false} & \text{otherwise}
     \end{array}\right. \\[4pt]
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, c) &=_{\mathit{def}}& c \\
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, \alpha@u) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\alpha) \wedge t \approx u \\
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, \lnot\alpha) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \lnot\,\mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\alpha) \\
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, \alpha\wedge\beta) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\alpha) \wedge \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\beta) \\
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, \alpha\vee\beta) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\alpha) \vee \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\beta) \\
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, \alpha\Rightarrow\beta) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\alpha) \Rightarrow \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\beta) \\
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, \forall x{:}D.\,\alpha) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \forall y{:}D.\,\mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\alpha[x:=y]) \\
   \mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\, \exists x{:}D.\,\alpha) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \exists y{:}D.\,\mathbf{Sat}(a@t,\alpha[x:=y])
   \end{array}

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(c) &=_{\mathit{def}}& [] \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\lnot\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi\wedge\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi)\,{+\!\!+}\,\mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi\vee\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi)\,{+\!\!+}\,\mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi\Rightarrow\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi)\,{+\!\!+}\,\mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}([\alpha]\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\forall x{:}D.\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Par}_{X,l\,{+\!\!+}\,[x{:}D]}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\exists x{:}D.\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{Par}_{X,l\,{+\!\!+}\,[x{:}D]}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(Y(d_f)) &=_{\mathit{def}}& [] \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\sigma Y(x_f{:}D_f{:=}d).\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \left\{\begin{array}{ll}
       l & \text{if } Y = X \\
       \mathbf{Par}_{X,l\,{+\!\!+}\,[x_f{:}D_f]}(\varphi) & \text{if } Y \neq X
     \end{array}\right. \\[4pt]
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\nabla(t)) &=_{\mathit{def}}& [] \\
   \mathbf{Par}_{X,l}(\Delta(t)) &=_{\mathit{def}}& []
   \end{array}

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{RHS}(c) &=_{\mathit{def}}& c \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi\wedge\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi) \wedge \mathbf{RHS}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi\vee\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi) \vee \mathbf{RHS}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\forall x{:}D.\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \forall x{:}D.\,\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\exists x{:}D.\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \exists x{:}D.\,\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}([\alpha]\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \begin{array}[t]{l}
       \bigwedge_{i\in I}\forall_{y:E_i}\bigl(
         \bigl(\mathbf{Sat}(a_i(f_i(x,y))@t_i(x,y),\alpha)
         \wedge c_i(x,y) \wedge t_i(x,y) > T\bigr) \\
       \quad\Rightarrow \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi)[T,x:=t_i(x,y),g_i(x,y)]\bigr)
     \end{array} \\[4pt]
   \mathbf{RHS}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \begin{array}[t]{l}
       \bigvee_{i\in I}\exists_{y:E_i}\bigl(
         \mathbf{Sat}(a_i(f_i(x,y))@t_i(x,y),\alpha)
         \wedge c_i(x,y) \wedge t_i(x,y) > T \\
       \quad\wedge\,\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi)[T,x:=t_i(x,y),g_i(x,y)]\bigr)
     \end{array} \\[4pt]
   \mathbf{RHS}(X(e)) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     X(T,\, e,\, x,\, \mathbf{Par}_{X,[]}(\varphi_0)) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\sigma X(x_f{:}D_f{:=}e).\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     X(T,\, e,\, x,\, \mathbf{Par}_{X,[]}(\varphi_0)) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\nabla(t)) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \left(\bigwedge_{i\in I\cup J}\forall_{y:E_i}
     (\lnot c_i(x,y) \vee t > t_i(x,y))\right) \wedge t > T \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\Delta(t)) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \left(\bigvee_{i\in I\cup J}\exists_{y:E_i}
     (c_i(x,y) \wedge t \leq t_i(x,y))\right) \vee t \leq T
   \end{array}

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{E}(c) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \epsilon \\
   \mathbf{E}(\varphi\wedge\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{E}(\varphi)\,\mathbf{E}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\varphi\vee\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{E}(\varphi)\,\mathbf{E}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\forall x{:}D.\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{E}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\exists x{:}D.\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{E}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{E}([\alpha]\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{E}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{E}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\nabla) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \epsilon \\
   \mathbf{E}(\nabla(t)) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \epsilon \\
   \mathbf{E}(\Delta) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \epsilon \\
   \mathbf{E}(\Delta(t)) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \epsilon \\
   \mathbf{E}(X(e)) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \epsilon \\
   \mathbf{E}(\sigma X(x_f{:}D_f{:=}e).\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \begin{array}[t]{l}
       \bigl(\sigma X(T{:}\mathbb{R},\; x_f{:}D_f,\; x{:}D_p,\;
       \mathbf{Par}_{X,[]}(\varphi_0)) = \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi)\bigr)\,
       \mathbf{E}(\varphi)
     \end{array}
   \end{array}

Counter example generation
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

There is a modified translation that adds information to the PBES from which a counter
example can be generated. Note that counter example generation is only available for the
untimed case. The function :math:`\mathbf{RHS}` is adapted from

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{RHS}([\alpha]\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \bigwedge_{i\in I}\forall_{y:E_i}\bigl(
       (\mathbf{Sat}(a_i(f_i(x,y)),\alpha) \wedge c_i(x,y))
       \Rightarrow \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi)[x:=g_i(x,y)]\bigr) \\[4pt]
   \mathbf{RHS}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \bigvee_{i\in I}\exists_{y:E_i}\bigl(
       (\mathbf{Sat}(a_i(f_i(x,y)),\alpha) \wedge c_i(x,y))
       \wedge \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi)[x:=g_i(x,y)]\bigr)
   \end{array}

into

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{RHS}([\alpha]\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \begin{array}[t]{l}
       \bigwedge_{i\in I}\forall_{y:E_i}
         \bigl(\mathbf{Sat}(a_i(f_i(x,y)),\alpha) \wedge c_i(x,y)\bigr)
         \Rightarrow \\
       \quad\bigl(\bigl(\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi)[x:=g_i(x,y)] \wedge
         Z_{a_i}^{+}(x,f_i(x,y),g_i(x,y))\bigr)
         \vee Z_{a_i}^{-}(x,f_i(x,y),g_i(x,y))\bigr)
     \end{array} \\[4pt]
   \mathbf{RHS}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \begin{array}[t]{l}
       \bigvee_{i\in I}\exists_{y:E_i}
         \bigl(\mathbf{Sat}(a_i(f_i(x,y)),\alpha) \wedge c_i(x,y)\bigr)
         \wedge \\
       \quad\bigl(\bigl(\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi)[x:=g_i(x,y)] \vee
         Z_{a_i}^{-}(x,f_i(x,y),g_i(x,y))\bigr)
         \wedge Z_{a_i}^{+}(x,f_i(x,y),g_i(x,y))\bigr)
     \end{array}
   \end{array}

where :math:`\nu Z_{a_i}^+(x,f_i(x,y),d') = \mathit{true}` and
:math:`\nu Z_{a_i}^-(x,f_i(x,y),d') = \mathit{false}` are additional equations for every
:math:`a_i` that appears in the LPS.

Transforming an LTS and a property to a PBES
---------------------------------------------

In this section we define the algorithm :math:`\operatorname{lts2pbes}` that generates a
PBES from a modal mu-calculus formula :math:`\varphi` and an LTS
:math:`\langle S, \mathit{Act}, \rightarrow, s_0 \rangle`:

.. math::

   \operatorname{lts2pbes}(\sigma X(d{:}D{:=}e).\,\varphi,\,
   \langle S, \mathit{Act}, {\rightarrow}, s_0 \rangle)
   = \langle \mathbf{E}_{\sigma X(d:D:=e).\,\varphi}(\varphi),\; X_{s_0}(e) \rangle

where the function :math:`\mathbf{E}` is inductively defined using the tables below. The
function :math:`\varphi` has to be in positive normal form, i.e. it may not contain any
:math:`\lnot` or :math:`\Rightarrow` symbols. A formula :math:`\psi` not of the form
:math:`\sigma X(d{:}D{:=}e).\,\varphi` is translated into :math:`\nu X().\,\psi`. We
denote the formula :math:`\varphi` that is passed to the algorithm
:math:`\operatorname{lts2pbes}` as :math:`\varphi_0`. It is used as a global variable in
the translation.

The functions :math:`\mathbf{Sat}` and :math:`\mathbf{Par}` are defined in the section
about :math:`\operatorname{lps2pbes}`.

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{E}(c) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \epsilon \\
   \mathbf{E}(\varphi\wedge\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{E}(\varphi)\,\mathbf{E}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\varphi\vee\psi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{E}(\varphi)\,\mathbf{E}(\psi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\forall x{:}D.\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{E}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\exists x{:}D.\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{E}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{E}([\alpha]\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{E}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \mathbf{E}(\varphi) \\
   \mathbf{E}(X(d)) &=_{\mathit{def}}& \epsilon \\
   \mathbf{E}(\sigma X(d{:}D{:=}e).\,\varphi) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \begin{array}[t]{l}
       \bigl(\sigma \{X_s(d{:}D,\, \mathbf{Par}_{X,[]}(\varphi_0)) =
       \mathbf{RHS}_\varphi(\varphi,s) \mid s \in S\}\bigr)\,\mathbf{E}(\varphi)
     \end{array}
   \end{array}

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{RHS}(c,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}& c \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi\wedge\psi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi,s) \wedge \mathbf{RHS}(\psi,s) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi\vee\psi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \mathbf{RHS}(\varphi,s) \vee \mathbf{RHS}(\psi,s) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\forall d{:}D.\,\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \forall d{:}D.\,\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi,s) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\exists d{:}D.\,\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \exists d{:}D.\,\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi,s) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}([\alpha]\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \bigwedge\bigl\{\mathbf{Sat}(a(x),\alpha) \Rightarrow
     \mathbf{RHS}_{\varphi_0}(\varphi,t)
     \mid s \overset{a(x)}{\rightarrow} t\bigr\} \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \bigvee\bigl\{\mathbf{Sat}(a(x),\alpha) \wedge
     \mathbf{RHS}_{\varphi_0}(\varphi,t)
     \mid s \overset{a(x)}{\rightarrow} t\bigr\} \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(X(e),\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     X_s(e,\, \mathbf{Par}_{X,[]}(\varphi_0)) \\
   \mathbf{RHS}(\sigma X(d{:}D{:=}e).\,\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     X_s(e,\, \mathbf{Par}_{X,[]}(\varphi_0))
   \end{array}

Counter example generation
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

There is a modified translation that adds information to the PBES from which a counter
example can be generated. The function :math:`\mathbf{RHS}` is adapted from

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{RHS}([\alpha]\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \bigwedge\bigl\{\mathbf{Sat}(a(x),\alpha) \Rightarrow
     \mathbf{RHS}_{\varphi_0}(\varphi,t)
     \mid s \overset{a(x)}{\rightarrow} t\bigr\} \\[4pt]
   \mathbf{RHS}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \bigvee\bigl\{\mathbf{Sat}(a(x),\alpha) \wedge
     \mathbf{RHS}_{\varphi_0}(\varphi,t)
     \mid s \overset{a(x)}{\rightarrow} t\bigr\}
   \end{array}

into

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lll}
   \mathbf{RHS}([\alpha]\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \bigwedge\bigl\{\mathbf{Sat}(a(x),\alpha) \Rightarrow
     \bigl((\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi,t) \wedge Z_a^+(s,x,t)) \vee Z_a^-(s,x,t)\bigr)
     \mid s \overset{a(x)}{\rightarrow} t\bigr\} \\[4pt]
   \mathbf{RHS}(\langle\alpha\rangle\varphi,\, s) &=_{\mathit{def}}&
     \bigvee\bigl\{\mathbf{Sat}(a(x),\alpha) \wedge
     \bigl((\mathbf{RHS}(\varphi,t) \vee Z_a^-(s,x,t)) \wedge Z_a^+(s,x,t)\bigr)
     \mid s \overset{a(x)}{\rightarrow} t\bigr\}
   \end{array}

where :math:`\nu Z_a^+(s,d_x,t) = \mathit{true}` and
:math:`\nu Z_a^-(s,d_x,t) = \mathit{false}` are additional equations for every :math:`a(x)`
that appears in the LTS.

Bisimulation
------------

Let

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lcl}
   M(d) &=& \sum_{i\in I_M}\sum_{e:E_i} c_i(d,e) \rightarrow a_i(d,e) \cdot M(g_i(d,e)) \\
   S(d) &=& \sum_{i\in I_S}\sum_{e:E_i} c_i(d,e) \rightarrow a_i(d,e) \cdot S(g_i(d,e))
   \end{array}

be two linear processes, such that :math:`I_M \cap I_S = \emptyset`. :math:`M` is called
the model and :math:`S` the specification. The expression :math:`a_i(d,e)` can be a
multiaction, or have the special value :math:`\tau`. We assume that there are no
:math:`\delta` summands. We define four PBES equation systems that express some kind of
bisimulation equivalence between :math:`M` and :math:`S`.

Branching bisimulation
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

:math:`\mathit{brbsim}(M,S) = \nu E_2\,\mu E_1`, where

.. math::

   \begin{array}{ccl}
   E_2 &:=& \{X^{M,S}(d,d') = \mathit{match}^{M,S}(d,d') \wedge \mathit{match}^{S,M}(d',d), \\
     & & \phantom{\{} X^{S,M}(d',d) = X^{M,S}(d,d')\} \\[4pt]
   E_1 &:=& \{Y_i^{M,S}(d,d',e) = \mathit{close}_i^{M,S}(d,d',e) \mid i\in I_M, \\
     & & \phantom{\{} Y_i^{S,M}(d',d,e) = \mathit{close}_i^{S,M}(d',d,e) \mid i\in I_S\}
   \end{array}

with for all :math:`i\in I_p` and :math:`(p,q)\in\{(M,S),(S,M)\}`:

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lcl}
   \mathit{match}^{p,q}(d,d') &=&
     \bigwedge_{i\in I_p}\forall_{e:E_i}.\bigl(c_i(d,e)
     \Rightarrow Y_i^{p,q}(d,d',e)\bigr) \\[4pt]
   \mathit{close}_i^{p,q}(d,d',e) &=&
     \begin{array}[t]{l}
       \bigvee_{\{j\in I_q \mid a_j=\tau\}}\exists_{e':E_j}.\bigl(c_j(d',e')
       \wedge Y_i^{p,q}(d,g_j(d',e'),e)\bigr) \\
       \vee\,\bigl(X^{p,q}(d,d') \wedge \mathit{step}_i^{p,q}(d,d',e)\bigr)
     \end{array} \\[8pt]
   \mathit{step}_i^{p,q}(d,d',e) &=&
     \left\{\begin{array}{cl}
       a_i = \tau{:} &
         \begin{array}[t]{l}
           X^{p,q}(g_i(d,e),d') \vee{} \\
           \bigvee_{\{j\in I_q \mid a_j=\tau\}}\exists_{e':E_j}.\bigl(c_j(d',e')
           \wedge X^{p,q}(g_i(d,e),g_j(d',e'))\bigr)
         \end{array} \\[8pt]
       a_i \neq \tau{:} &
         \bigvee_{\{j\in I_q \mid a_j=a_i\}}\exists_{e':E_j}.\bigl(c_j(d',e')
         \wedge (a_i(d,e) = a_j(d',e'))
         \wedge X^{p,q}(g_i(d,e),g_j(d',e'))\bigr)
     \end{array}\right.
   \end{array}

Strong bisimulation
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

:math:`\mathit{sbisim}(M,S) = \nu E`, where

.. math::

   \begin{array}{ccl}
   E &:=& \{X^{M,S}(d,d') = \mathit{match}^{M,S}(d,d') \wedge \mathit{match}^{S,M}(d',d), \\
     & & \phantom{\{} X^{S,M}(d',d) = X^{M,S}(d,d')\}
   \end{array}

with for all :math:`i\in I_p` and :math:`(p,q)\in\{(M,S),(S,M)\}`:

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lcl}
   \mathit{match}^{p,q}(d,d') &=&
     \bigwedge_{i\in I_p}\forall_{e:E_i}.\bigl(c_i(d,e)
     \Rightarrow \mathit{step}_i^{p,q}(d,d',e)\bigr) \\[4pt]
   \mathit{step}_i^{p,q}(d,d',e) &=&
     \bigvee_{j\in I_q}\exists_{e':E_j}.\bigl(c_j(d',e')
     \wedge (a_i(d,e) = a_j(d',e'))
     \wedge X^{p,q}(g_i(d,e),g_j(d',e'))\bigr)
   \end{array}

Weak bisimulation
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

:math:`\mathit{wbisim}(M,S) = \nu E_2\,\mu E_1`, where

.. math::

   \begin{array}{ccl}
   E_3 &:=& \{X^{M,S}(d,d') = \mathit{match}^{M,S}(d,d') \wedge \mathit{match}^{S,M}(d',d), \\
     & & \phantom{\{} X^{S,M}(d',d) = X^{M,S}(d,d')\} \\[4pt]
   E_2 &:=& \{Y_{1,i}^{M,S}(d,d',e) = \mathit{close}_{1,i}^{M,S}(d,d',e) \mid i\in I_M, \\
     & & \phantom{\{} Y_{2,i}^{M,S}(d,d') = \mathit{close}_{2,i}^{M,S}(d,d') \mid i\in I_M, \\
     & & \phantom{\{} Y_{1,i}^{S,M}(d',d,e) = \mathit{close}_{1,i}^{S,M}(d',d,e) \mid i\in I_S, \\
     & & \phantom{\{} Y_{2,i}^{S,M}(d',d) = \mathit{close}_{2,i}^{S,M}(d',d) \mid i\in I_S\}
   \end{array}

with for all :math:`i\in I_p` and :math:`(p,q)\in\{(M,S),(S,M)\}`:

.. math::

   \begin{array}{lcl}
   \mathit{match}^{p,q}(d,d') &=&
     \bigwedge_{i\in I_p}\forall_{e:E_i}.\bigl(c_i(d,e)
     \Rightarrow Y_{1,i}^{p,q}(d,d',e)\bigr) \\[4pt]
   \mathit{close}_{1,i}^{p,q}(d,d',e) &=&
     \begin{array}[t]{l}
       \left(\bigvee_{\{j\in I_q \mid a_j=\tau\}}\exists_{e':E_j}.\bigl(c_j(d',e')
       \wedge Y_{1,i}^{p,q}(d,g_j(d',e'),e)\bigr)\right)
       \vee\,\mathit{step}_i^{p,q}(d,d',e)
     \end{array} \\[8pt]
   \mathit{step}_i^{p,q}(d,d',e) &=&
     \left\{\begin{array}{cl}
       a_i = \tau{:} & \mathit{close}_{2,i}^{p,q}(g_i(d,e),d') \\[4pt]
       a_i \neq \tau{:} &
         \bigvee_{j\in I_q}\exists_{e':E_j}.\bigl(c_j(d',e')
         \wedge a_i(d,e) = a_j(d',e')
         \wedge \mathit{close}_{2,i}^{p,q}(g_i(d,e),g_j(d',e'))\bigr)
     \end{array}\right. \\[8pt]
   \mathit{close}_{2,i}^{p,q}(d,d') &=&
     X^{p,q}(d,d') \vee
     \bigvee_{\{j\in I_q \mid a_j=\tau\}}\exists_{e':E_j}.\bigl(c_j(d',e')
     \wedge Y_{2,i}^{p,q}(d,g_j(d',e'))\bigr)
   \end{array}

Branching simulation equivalence
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

:math:`\mathit{brsimeq}(M,S) = \nu E_2\,\mu E_1`, where

.. math::

   \begin{array}{ccl}
   E_2 &:=& \{X^{M,S}(d,d') = \mathit{match}^{M,S}(d,d') \wedge \mathit{match}^{S,M}(d',d), \\
     & & \phantom{\{} X^{M,S}(d,d') = X^{S,M}(d',d), \\
     & & \phantom{\{} X^{S,M}(d',d) = X^{M,S}(d,d')\} \\[4pt]
   E_1 &:=& \{Y_i^{M,S}(d,d',e) = \mathit{close}_i^{M,S}(d,d',e) \mid i\in I_M, \\
     & & \phantom{\{} Y_i^{S,M}(d',d,e) = \mathit{close}_i^{S,M}(d',d,e) \mid i\in I_S\}
   \end{array}

with :math:`\mathit{match}`, :math:`\mathit{close}`, and :math:`\mathit{step}` defined
exactly the same as in branching bisimulation.
